How to download your Instagram data

1Open the Instagram app → Menu (☰) → Settings and privacy.

2Go to Account Center → Your information and permissions → Download your information.

3Select your Instagram account. Tap Some of your information → search/select Followers and following.

4At the bottom, set Date range to All time, choose Format: JSON, and tap Create files.

Tip: JSON is required (not HTML). The app reads JSON only.

1Open instagram.com in a browser → More (left sidebar) → Settings.

2Go to Accounts Center → Your information and permissions → Download your information.

3Choose your IG account → Some of your information → select Followers and following.

4Set Date range to All time • Format: JSON • then Create files.

What you’ll receive

Instagram emails you a link to download a .zip file. When you unzip it, look for the JSON files below (names may vary slightly):

• followers_1.json (sometimes followers_2.json, followers_3.json…)
• following.json
Common locations in the export:
• followers_and_following/followers_1.json
• followers_and_following/following.json
or (older exports)
• connections/followers_1.json
• connections/following.json

If you see multiple followers_*.json files, that’s normal—Instagram splits large lists. The app merges them automatically.

Use them in UnfollowGuard

  1. Open the app: unfollowguard.com/app.html
  2. Drag in all followers_*.json files and following.json (or click to select).
  3. Click Compare Now.
  4. (Optional) Add brands/creators to your Whitelist so they don’t show up in results.
  5. Export both lists to clean text files.

Troubleshooting

  • No results? Make sure you selected the JSON format when creating the export, not HTML.
  • Still zipped? Unzip the downloaded file first, then drag the JSON files into the app.
  • Different file names? That’s okay—look for any files that clearly contain followers or following in JSON.
  • Privacy check: the app never uploads your files; everything runs locally.
